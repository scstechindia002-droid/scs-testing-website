Revolutionizing Power Management with a Centralized Monitoring System

To modernize the business operations, MAHAGENCO undertook a landmark initiative to implement a Centralized Monitoring System (CMS) across its thermal power plants, transforming the way power generation is monitored, optimized, and managed.

The business challenge

Operating across 8 major thermal power stations and generating over 10,000 MW, the utility faced critical challenges:

01
Disjointed Monitoring Systems
Each plant operated in silos, with localized tools offering limited visibility to headquarters and no unified decision-making view.
02
Reactive Maintenance and Downtime
Without predictive analytics, unplanned failures and suboptimal asset performance led to reduced machine availability and higher O&M costs.
03
Manual Performance Tracking
Critical operational parameters were tracked manually or via legacy systems, leading to inconsistencies and delayed analysis.
04
Lack of Central Intelligence
There was no centralized dashboard to correlate performance trends, benchmark efficiency, or inform real-time operational decisions across plants.

Our Solutions

To overcome these challenges, SCS Tech and STEAG Energy Services jointly implemented a cutting-edge CMS platform—unifying performance, diagnostics, forecasting, and reporting into one intelligent ecosystem.

Performance Analysis & Diagnostics

Real-time monitoring of boiler, turbine, and auxiliary equipment across plants with intelligent alerts on deviation and inefficiencies.

Integrated Dashboards & Visualization

HQ-based dashboard visualizes critical KPIs such as load, MS temp/pressure, turbine efficiency, and fuel flow in one consolidated view.

Predictive Analytics Engine

Early Warning System (SPC-based) identifies failure patterns before they occur, enabling proactive maintenance and minimizing downtime.

Boiler Lifetime Monitoring

Real-time thermal stress analysis extends boiler asset life and flags mechanical fatigue risks based on live metal temperature data.

Digital Debriefing System

Event recording, fault diagnosis, and root cause analysis are systematized—building a historical knowledge base for training and SOP refinement.

Centralized CMS Infrastructure

Cloud-connected servers, video walls, and data redundancy via SAN/NAS ensure reliable, 24x7 access to performance data from all power stations.

Key Scope of Work Done

CMS Rollout Across 8 Plants + HQ

Centralized Monitoring System deployed across thermal plants and headquarters for unified visibility.

Integrated Performance & Analytics Suite

Modules for performance tracking, predictive analytics, boiler health, fuel use, and merit order rating.

SCADA & Real-Time Data Integration

Live data capture via OPC-DA interfacing with SCADA systems.

High-Availability Data & DR Setup

Central SAN/NAS data center with disaster recovery for uninterrupted operations.

Live Dashboards & Incident Debriefing

70” video walls with advanced alarm/event analysis tools.

Secure Connectivity & O&M Support

MPLS/P2P networking, cross-device access, training, and full facility management.

Impact

  • Real-Time Visibility Across the Fleet: Live plant performance metrics accessible to HQ for operational benchmarking and cross-station coordination.
  • Predictive Decision-Making: AI/ML-driven early warnings reduce unplanned downtime, improve asset reliability, and optimize maintenance windows.
  • Improved Generation Efficiency: Set-point and soot blowing optimization reduce fuel costs, enhance thermal performance, and support DSM alignment.
  • Scalable, Future-Ready Infrastructure : Built to support future modules, including AI forecasting, renewable integration, and centralized dispatch control.
